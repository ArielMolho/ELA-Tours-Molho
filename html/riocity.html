Rio like a local

See a different side of Rio de Janeiro as we explore the magical city on foot and with local transportation and our local expert guide. In this way, you'll gain so much more insight into the tradition and culture of this wonderful city as we meander our way through the city, walking past the small shops and restaurants lined outside the trodden touristy path. We'll meet in front of the famous "Copacabana Palace", after learning a bit about the history of this iconic hotel during Copacabana's 'golden years' during the 1930's & 1940's. Then walk a few blocks to get to the Subway Metro Station, Carioca's (those native to Rio) preferred mode of everyday transport around the city, to visit the Municipal Theatre, located in the city center and built on an eclectic style at the first of the 20th century.
A few blocks away, we'll jump on the Bondinho Santa Teresa, a cable car built in 1872 that runs on the top of an old aqueduct called “Arcos da Lapa”. After few stations, we reach the bohemian neighborhood of Santa Teresa, here we'll meander the streets filled with old antique mansions, also visiting Parque das Ruinas (closed Mondays), an old Palace of ruins rebuilt to become the center of many art and cultural activities in the area. On a clear day this vantage point hosts spectacular panoramic views over the city center, Sugar Loaf, and Guanabara Bay. A few “Kodak” moments later, we'll arrive at the colorful Celaron Stairs, a must see cultural local artist masterpiece in the neighborhood of Lapa.
Before the day comes to an end, we'll stop to enjoy the Confiteria Colombo, an old Art Nouveau coffee shop from the late 1800's, chosen as one of the ten most beautiful coffee shops in the world (closed Sundays). Afterwards, we can either drop you at the Sugarloaf Mountain to grab a cable car to the top, or we can take the metro back to the Copacabana Palace where the tour ends. If you'd like to head to Christ the Redeemer statue, our guide will share with you directions on how to get there on your own in the afternoon, it's best to pre-purchase entrance fees in advance, as availability can be little during some periods.
